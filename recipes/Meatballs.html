Algerian Kefta

How to Make Algerian Kefta

Making Algerian Kefta can be time-consuming, but the results are well worth the wait.

You'll find a detailed ingredient list and step-by-step instructions in the recipe below, but let's go over the basics:

Ingredients

  • 1 pound lean ground beef
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced, divided
  • ¼ cup finely chopped onion, divided
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 roma (plum) tomatoes, diced
  • 1 teaspoon dried parsley
  • ½ cup water

How to Make Algerian Kefta Step-By-Step

Here's a very brief overview of what you can expect when you make homemade Algerian Kefta:

  1. Combine the ground beef with half of the minced garlic and a tablespoon of chopped onion.
  2. Mix with your hands until fully incorporated.
  3. Shape the meat mixture into 1 1/2-inch oblong patties; you should have 12 to 14 meatballs.
  4. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat.
  5. Brown the patties in batches until they are crispy on both sides and are no longer pink in the center, about 10 minutes
  6. Reduce the heat to medium and stir in the remaining chopped onion.
  7. Add salt and pepper.
  8. Cook the onions in the drippings, stirring constantly, until the onion has softened and turned translucent, about 5 minutes.
  9. Stir in the roma tomatoes, dried parsley, ras el hanout, and water.
  10. Cook until the tomatoes are soft, about 5 minutes.
  11. Pour the tomato sauce over the meatballs and serve.
